About Linton

Linton is running for the Board Member position in District 9 which consists of Youngsville, Milton, and a portion of Broussard.

His educational background in Accounting and Finance as well as his experience as a pastor and a substitute teacher for Lafayette Parish Schools will aid him in fighting for what's best for all students, especially those in District 9.

Linton is married to Rachel Lacy Broussard and together they have 3 sons, two of which are currently enrolled in the Lafayette Parish School System.

Ways That Linton Has Served Our Community:
  • Current Lafayette Parish School System Substitute Teacher (Employed January 2021)
  • Member of the Superintendent’s Parent Advisory Panel
  • Member of the Ernest Gallet Elementary School Parent Advisory Council
  • Current Board Member for the Acadiana Churches Disaster Network
  • Current Member of Acadiana VOAD
  • Cajun Rush Youth Soccer Coach
  • Assisted Coaching staff of FNA Flag Football Team
  • Former Acadiana VOAD Faith Committee Chair
  • Former Louisiana Open Golf Tournament Volunteer - 7 Years
  • Zurich Classic Golf Tournament Volunteer - April 2022
  • October 2021 Bus Driver Appreciation Day Volunteer
  • Previous United Way Adopt-A-Teacher Volunteer

The future of Lafayette Parish depends on an education system that provides the best opportunity for students and educators to thrive and feel accomplished every single day of the school year.

I will work to...

Ensure all students succeed

Ensure that all students are given the best opportunity to learn in an environment that provides the ability to grow and learn according to their needs.

Prioritize the budget

Create a budget that ensures that our tax dollars are used in the best way possible.

Improve teacher retention rates

Create healthy environments where both teachers and administration feel supported and properly compensated for their work.

Provide proper athletic facilities

Provide the proper facilities and equipment as appropriate for different schools and grade levels.

Simplify the dress code

Create a dress code policy that is easy to understand and comply with across all grade levels.

Improve bus transportation

Create additional opportunities to train and employ new full time and part time drivers.

Increase community support of schools

Engage families and businesses to actively participate in school activities and programs

When families win, our communities win!  It starts with a great education!
